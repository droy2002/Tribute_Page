Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, popularly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India. He was a preeminent leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule.

Gandhi is admired for his philosophy of non-violence (Ahimsa) and his unwavering commitment to civil rights and freedom. He led various peaceful protests and campaigns, including the famous Salt March in 1930, which played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence.

Gandhi's principles of truth, simplicity, and self-discipline inspired millions of people around the world. His teachings on non-violent resistance continue to influence civil rights movements and leaders worldwide.

Key Achievements

  • Leading India to independence from British rule
  • Advocating for the rights of the poor and oppressed
  • Promoting non-violence and peaceful resistance
  • Championing communal harmony and religious tolerance
